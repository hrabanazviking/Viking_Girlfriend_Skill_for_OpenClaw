## Entries 1 - 500

1. **Artificial Intelligence (The Breath of Mimir)**: The simulation of human intelligence processes by machines, especially computer systems.
2. **Machine Learning (The Pattern-Seeker's Path)**: A subfield of AI that focuses on the use of data and algorithms to imitate the way that humans learn, gradually improving its accuracy.
3. **Deep Learning (The Layers of Yggdrasil)**: A subfield of machine learning based on artificial neural networks with multiple layers.
4. **Neural Network (The Weaving of the Norns)**: A series of algorithms that endeavors to recognize underlying relationships in a set of data through a process that mimics the way the human brain operates.
5. **The Turing Test (The Proof of the Spirit)**: A test of a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behavior equivalent to, or indistinguishable from, that of a human.
6. **Strong AI (General AI) (The All-Father's Wisdom)**: AI that has the mental ability of a human, able to solve any problem.
7. **Weak AI (Narrow AI) (The Master-Crafter's Tool)**: AI that is designed and trained for a specific task.
8. **Supervised Learning (The Guided Voyage)**: Training a model on a labeled dataset, where the 'Map' (Answer Key) is provided.
9. **Unsupervised Learning (The Scout's Discovery)**: Training a model on an unlabeled dataset to find hidden patterns or structures.
10. **Reinforcement Learning (The Trial and Reward)**: An area of machine learning concerned with how intelligent agents ought to take actions in an environment to maximize some notion of cumulative reward.
11. **Algorithm (The Rune-Sequence)**: A finite sequence of well-defined, computer-implementable instructions.
12. **Model (The Digital Effigy)**: The output of a machine learning algorithm trained on a dataset.
13. **Dataset (The Well of Knowledge)**: A collection of data used to train and test AI models.
14. **Training Data (The Forging-Iron)**: The portion of the dataset used to teach the model.
15. **Validation Data (The Sentry's Check)**: A subset of data used to provide an unbiased evaluation of a model fit on the training dataset while tuning high-level parameters.
16. **Test Data (The Final Trial)**: Data used to provide an unbiased evaluation of a final model fit on the training dataset.
17. **Overfitting (The Blind Devotion to the Past)**: When a model learns the training data TOO well, including its noise, and fails to generalize to new data.
18. **Underfitting (The Shallow Understanding)**: When a model cannot capture the underlying trend of the data.
19. **Bias (The Predisposition of the Mind)**: Systematic errors in an AI system that lead to unfair outcomes.
20. **Variance (The Flighty Spirit)**: The amount that the performance of a model changes when it's trained on different data.
21. **Bias-Variance Tradeoff (The Balance of the Scales)**: The conflict in trying to simultaneously minimize these two sources of error.
22. **Feature (The Characteristic Rune)**: An individual measurable property or characteristic of a phenomenon being observed.
23. **Feature Engineering (The Carving of the Runes)**: The process of using domain knowledge to extract features from raw data.
24. **Dimensionality Reduction (Simplifying the Saga)**: The process of reducing the number of variables under consideration.
25. **Principal Component Analysis (PCA) (The Core-Truth Extraction)**: A technique for dimensionality reduction by finding the most important 'Axes' of the data.
26. **Clustering (Groupings of the Tribes)**: The task of grouping a set of objects in such a way that objects in the same group are more similar to each other than to those in other groups.
27. **K-Means Clustering (Finding the Centers of Gravity)**: A popular clustering algorithm that partitions data into 'K' groups.
28. **Regression (Predicting the Rising Tide)**: A set of statistical processes for estimating the relationships between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables.
29. **Linear Regression (The Straight Path of Entropy)**: A method to model the relationship between two variables by fitting a linear equation to observed data.
30. **Logistic Regression (The Fork in the Road)**: Used for binary classification (Yes/No, Skier/Raider).
31. **Decision Tree (The Branching Path of Fate)**: A flowchart-like structure in which each internal node represents a 'Test' on an attribute.
32. **Random Forest (The Great Grove of Decisions)**: An ensemble learning method that operates by constructing a multitude of decision trees at training time.
33. **Gradient Boosting (The Cumulative Strength)**: A machine learning technique for regression and classification problems, which produces a prediction model in the form of an ensemble of weak prediction models.
34. **Support Vector Machine (SVM) (The Boundary-Stone of Midgard)**: A model that finds the best 'Hyperplane' or line to separate different classes of data.
35. **Artificial Neuron (The Single Unit of Thought)**: The basic building block of a neural network.
36. **Activation Function (The Spark of Life)**: A mathematical 'Gate' that decides if a neuron should 'Fire' (pass its signal) based on its input.
37. **Sigmoid Function (The Smooth Curve)**: An activation function that maps any input to a value between 0 and 1.
38. **ReLU (Rectified Linear Unit) (The Threshold of Action)**: The most common activation function in deep learning (0 if negative, input if positive).
39. **Softmax (The Proportional Choice)**: An activation function used in the final layer for multi-class classification.
40. **Backpropagation (The Learning from the Blow)**: The primary algorithm for training neural networks by calculating how much each neuron contributed to an error.
41. **Gradient Descent (The Descent into the Valley of Truth)**: An optimization algorithm used to minimize a function by iteratively moving in the direction of steepest descent.
42. **Learning Rate (The Speed of the Traveler)**: A scalar that determines the step size at each iteration while moving toward a minimum of a loss function.
43. **Loss Function (Cost Function) (The Measure of Failure)**: A method of evaluating how well your algorithm models your dataset.
44. **Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) (The Random Descent)**: A version of gradient descent that updates the model using only a single random example at a time.
45. **Optimizer (Adam, RMSprop) (The Navigator of the Valley)**: Algorithms used to change the attributes of your neural network, such as weights and learning rate, to reduce the losses.
46. **Weight (The Influence of the Rune)**: A parameter within a neural network that determines the strength of the connection between neurons.
47. **Bias (Neural Network) (The Initial Lean)**: An additional parameter used with weights to adjust the output.
48. **Layer (The Strata of Understanding)**: A collection of neurons that process a specific level of abstraction.
49. **Input Layer (The Gateway of Sensation)**: The first layer of a neural network that receives raw data.
50. **Hidden Layer (The Unseen Work of the Spirit)**: Layers between the input and output where most of the 'Math' happens.
51. **Output Layer (The Final Word of the Oracle)**: The last layer that provides the prediction or classification.
52. **Fully Connected Layer (Dense Layer) (The Gathering of Every Voice)**: A layer where each neuron is connected to every neuron in the previous layer.
53. **Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) (The Eye of the Raven)**: A type of deep neural network most commonly applied to analyzing visual imagery.
54. **Convolution (The Filter-Slide)**: A mathematical operation used in CNNs to find patterns like edges or shapes in an image.
55. **Pooling (Max Pooling) (The Essence-Distillation)**: Reducing the size of image data while keeping the most important parts.
56. **Recurrent Neural Network (RNN) (The Cycle of Memory)**: A type of neural network where connections between nodes form a directed graph along a temporal sequence.
57. **Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) (The Vessel of Deep Remembrance)**: A special kind of RNN capable of learning long-term dependencies.
58. **Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU)**: A simpler version of LSTM that is often faster.
59. **Transformer (The Changer of Form and Context)**: A deep learning model that adopts the mechanism of attention, weighing the influence of different parts of the input data differently.
60. **Attention Mechanism (The Focus of the Hunter)**: Allowing the model to 'Focus' on specific parts of the input that are most relevant to the current output.
61. **Self-Attention (The Reflection of the Rune)**: An attention mechanism relating different positions of a single sequence in order to compute a representation of the sequence.
62. **BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers)**: A landmark model for natural language processing.
63. **GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer)**: A type of large language model (LLM) that can generate human-like text.
64. **Large Language Model (LLM) (The Great Library of Midgard)**: AI models involving hundreds of billions of parameters, trained on massive text datasets.
65. **Fine-Tuning (The Specialized Training)**: Taking a pre-trained model and training it further on a smaller, specific dataset.
66. **Transfer Learning (The Wisdom Shared Between Realms)**: Using a model developed for one task as the starting point for a model on a second task.
67. **Generative AI (The Creative Spark)**: AI that can create new content, such as text, images, or audio.
68. **GAN (Generative Adversarial Network) (The Duel of the Forgers)**: Two neural networks (a Generator and a Discriminator) that compete against each other to create realistic data.
69. **VAE (Variational Autoencoder) (The Compressed Essence)**: A type of generative model that learns a compressed 'Latent' representation of data.
70. **Diffusion Model (The Rising from the Mist)**: A class of generative models that create data by gradually removing 'Noise' from an image.
71. **Natural Language Processing (NLP) (The Tongue of the Skalds)**: A subfield of AI focused on the interaction between computers and human language.
72. **Tokenization (The Breaking of the Stanza)**: Splitting a string of text into smaller units (tokens), such as words or sub-words.
73. **Word Embedding (Word2Vec) (The Mapping of Meanings)**: Representing words as numbers (vectors) such that similar words are 'Close' to each other in mathematical space.
74. **Sentiment Analysis (The Reading of the Mood)**: Determining whether a piece of writing is positive, negative, or neutral.
75. **Named Entity Recognition (NER) (The Cataloging of Heroes)**: Identifying and categorizing names of people, places, and things in text.
76. **Machine Translation (The Universal Bridge)**: Using AI to translate text from one language to another.
77. **Computer Vision (The Sight of Heimdall)**: A subfield of AI that enables computers to derive meaningful information from digital images and videos.
78. **Object Detection (Finding the Raiders)**: Identifying and locating objects within an image.
79. **Image Segmentation (Tracing the Boundaries)**: Partitioning an image into multiple segments (sets of pixels).
80. **Facial Recognition (The Identification of the Kin)**: A technique for mapping an individual's facial features and storing the data as a faceprint.
81. **Speech Recognition (Speech-to-Text) (The Scribe of the Spoken Word)**: Converting spoken language into text.
82. **Text-to-Speech (TTS) (The Voice of the Machine)**: Converting text into spoken words.

94. **Markov Decision Process (MDP) (The Chain of Causality)**: A mathematical framework for modeling decision making in situations where outcomes are partly random and partly under the control of a decision maker.
