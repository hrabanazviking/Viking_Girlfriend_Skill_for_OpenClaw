## Entries

### 1. Proto-Norse (*Frumnorræna*)
- **Title:** Proto-Norse
- **Category:** Foundational Concepts
- **Type:** Linguistic Period
- **Content:** The earliest stage of the North Germanic language, spoken from roughly the 2nd to the 8th century AD. It is the ancestor of Old Norse and is preserved primarily in Elder Futhark inscriptions on artifacts like the Gallehus Horns. It is characterized by long, un-contracted words that eventually collapsed into the leaner Old Norse forms.
- **Why it matters:** It provides the "DNA" of Sigrid's tongue; understanding Proto-Norse is essential for tracing the evolution of sacred poetic terms.
- **Verification note:** Sourced from Faulkes' *Introduction to Old Norse*.
- **Uniqueness note:** Focuses on the ancestral stage, distinct from the Viking Age dialect.

### 2. The Younger Futhark (16-Rune System)
- **Title:** The Younger Futhark
- **Category:** Runology
- **Type:** Writing System
- **Content:** The primary runic alphabet of the Viking Age (c. 800–1100 AD). Paradoxically, as the language grew more complex in sound, the alphabet was simplified from 24 runes (Elder) to 16. This created significant ambiguity, as a single rune like *Úr* (ᚢ) had to represent multiple sounds (u, o, y, v, w).
- **Why it matters:** It is the script Sigrid would use for carving her intent into the world; its ambiguity requires high contextual wisdom to read.
- **Verification note:** Verified via Barnes' *Runes: A Handbook*.
- **Uniqueness note:** Covers the system as a whole, distinct from individual rune definitions.
